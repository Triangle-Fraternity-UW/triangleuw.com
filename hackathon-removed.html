What is Triangle Hacks?

February 16, 2019 marks the first ever Triangle Hacks, a 12-hour hackathon meant to provide a venue for UW students to express their creativity and showcase their technical skills. Regardless of experience, Triangle Hacks is open to all UW students! Don't be afraid of coming if you don't know much, we'll have workshops geared towards students of all experience levels.

Projects developed at TriangleHacks will revolve around three distinct tracks: Innovation, Altruism, and Entertainment!

Innovation

Move and shake. Entries in the innovation category will be creative, cutting-edge solutions to modern problems.

Altruism

If you love helping people, the altruism category is for you. Entries in this category will be geared towards providing some form of humanitarian benefit to a community/communities.

Entertainment

Build something that captivates people. Whether it's a game, some sort of social media, or something people will have a good time using, it fits in the entertainment category.

FAQ

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where small groups of individuals collaborate to solve a problem using technology.

Who’s allowed to attend?

Any UW student is allowed to participate! Please remember to bring your husky id to the event.

Do I have to know how to code?

Nope! You do not have to know how to code to participate in the hackathon. If you are interested in design applications, graphic design, or managing a team, there will be a spot for you on a team. If you do not want to participate in the competition, there will be workshops for you to attend to learn more about computing.

How much does admission cost?

Nothing at all. Admission is free to all attendees! Food and drinks will be provided as well.

What should I bring?

You should bring yourself, your husky id, a laptop, any peripherals you may need, chargers, pencils, and paper.

Can I build on a previous project?

No. You may come in with an idea for a project but you cannot build off of a previous project from another hackathon or one you are working on at home. This will give you an advantage over other teams which doesn’t make the competition very fair.

How big are teams?

You’re allowed to work individually (although we recommend working with a team) or with a team of up to four people.

How do I create or get on a team?

Although a lot of hackers will come with a team already formed, Don’t worry if you don’t have a team formed going into the event. We’ll be holding a small mixer early on to form teams of students with similar ideas. Who knows, you might meet a group of people who become your lifelong friends!

Do I have to be in a computing major to participate?

No, you do not have to be already in - or even applying to - a computing major. If you have an interest in programming, designing applications, graphic design, or managing a team, you will have a great time at a hackathon.

How competitive will this hackathon be?

Hackathons are not just about the competition. They’re events for people interested in technology to meet new friends, strengthen current friendships, and improve their skills and knowledge in their field of interest. Yes, there will be a panel of judges critiquing every project submitted by the end of the event, but in the end, nobody is there to judge you if you lose.

Do I get anything for winning?

Yes! We won’t be revealing what they are before the event but we’ll have some cool prizes for you to win.
